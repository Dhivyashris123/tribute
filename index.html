Tribute to Subash Chandra Bose

Subash Chandra Bose, also known as Netaji, was a prominent Indian nationalist leader who played a key role in the country's struggle for independence from British rule. He was born on January 23, 1897, and is remembered for his unwavering commitment to the cause of freedom.

Bose believed in the motto, "Give me blood, and I will give you freedom," and he led the Indian National Army (INA) to fight alongside the Axis Powers during World War II, with the hope of liberating India. His leadership and determination continue to inspire generations of Indians.

Subash Chandra Bose's legacy lives on, and his contributions to India's independence movement are celebrated to this day. He remains a symbol of courage and determination in the quest for freedom.
